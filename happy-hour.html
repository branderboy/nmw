[ What Happy Hour is ]

Two hours that decide the night.

Doors open at 6 PM. The main showcase starts at 7. In the two hours between, the room compresses: artists meet press, sponsors meet artists, DJs hear early cuts, fans get the first listen. It's the highest-leverage part of the night, and we keep it small on purpose.

Meet & Greet

Artists, A&Rs, DJs, sponsors, all in one room before the music starts.

Showcase Set

Three audition-approved artists run unreleased material in front of a tasting room.

Content Capture

Editorial team shoots clips and interviews for press, blog, and partner distribution.

Press Mingle

Digiwaxx, DSN, FUBU Radio, HLM, QueueVision rotate through the room.

[ The run-of-show ]

6 PM to 8 PM.

  1. 6:00 PM

    Doors + walk-up mingle

    RSVPs check in. Drink in hand by 6:05.

  2. 6:20 PM

    Hosted introductions

    Hosts pull the room together. The week's audition-approved artists step forward.

  3. 6:35 PM

    Showcase set #1

    First artist runs a tight 12-minute set. Unreleased material encouraged.

  4. 7:00 PM

    Press pit interviews

    Rotating media partners pull artists for short on-camera segments.

  5. 7:25 PM

    Showcase set #2 + #3

    Back-to-back sets. DJ in the booth, A&R in the corner.

  6. 7:55 PM

    Roll into the main show

    Happy Hour ends, the headline NMW set begins. Most happy hour RSVPs stay through.

[ Why the content team loves it ]

One hidden event, fifty pieces of content.

Happy Hour is a content factory disguised as a mixer. Every Wednesday produces blog posts ("Things to do in Manhattan on a Wednesday"), short-form clips, artist showcase reels, interview snippets, and programmatic listings on DICE, Eventbrite, Bandsintown, Luma, Songkick, and TripAdvisor. The room is the moment; the moment becomes the demand signal.
